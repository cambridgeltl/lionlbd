World AIDS Day 1994: AIDS and the family. WAD 1993 spurs global commitment.
A multitude of activities took place December 1, 1993, to raise awareness about AIDS and foster commitment against the pandemic. At the United Nations headquarters in New York, the Secretary General pledged to do more about AIDS and appealed for broad political support from the rest of the international community. Liza Minelli sang and the Executive Director of UNICEF spoke. The day of events at World Health Organization headquarters in Geneva included the signing of a Declaration on AIDS by the Director-General and Regional Directors, as well as music and theater performances. Other activities are also described in Argentina, England, Tanzania, the US, Egypt, Nepal, Hungary, Sudan, the Philippines, Slovenia, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Pakistan, Rwanda, and elsewhere.
